About the Course

The Basic to Advanced Excel Course is designed to help students, professionals, business owners, and working executives master Microsoft Excel from fundamental concepts to advanced data analysis and automation techniques.

This course provides practical hands-on training to improve productivity, data management, reporting, and analytical skills using real-world business scenarios. Participants will learn how to efficiently use Excel tools for calculations, data visualization, reporting, dashboards, and automation.

Complete coverage from basic spreadsheets to advanced VBA automation in one course

Industry-relevant training for data analysis, reporting, and business intelligence

Practical training with real-world business scenarios and case studies

Opens doors to careers as Data Analyst, MIS Executive, Business Analyst, and more

Course Modules

6 focused modules covering Excel fundamentals through advanced automation

1

Introduction to Microsoft Excel

Introduces the Excel interface, workbook management, and essential spreadsheet operations — including data entry, formatting, basic calculations, and file management.

2

Excel Formulas and Functions

Covers mathematical, logical, text, date & time, and lookup functions — including IF, AND, OR, VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, and conditional formatting.

3

Data Management and Analysis

Teaches data organization, filtering, sorting, and analysis techniques — including data validation, removing duplicates, Pivot Tables, and Pivot Charts.

4

Charts and Dashboard Creation

Covers data visualization and interactive dashboard development — including Excel charts, combo charts, sparklines, KPI visualization, and interactive reporting.

5

Advanced Excel Tools

Introduces advanced features used in business analytics — including INDEX & MATCH, Power Query basics, What-If Analysis, Goal Seek, Solver, and advanced conditional formatting.

6

Excel Automation with Macros & VBA

Teaches automation techniques — including recording macros, VBA basics, automating reports, building user forms, and error handling to reduce repetitive tasks.
